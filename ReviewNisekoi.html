Nisekoi

A review By : Ruzchel LLoyd Estrella

    Raku Ichijou is a self-proclaimed honor student who strives to become a civil servant because he does not want to be the succesor of his father, a head of the yakuza. He one day crosses paths with a blonde girl, named Chitoge Kirisaki, who knees him in the face for their first meeting. He then later found out that she is the daughter of a mafia and that they will be forced to play lovers in order to stop a war between the yakuza and the mafia. What will happen to their lives as highschool students now?

    Personally, This is one of the best shows for me as it is the first anime of its genre that I have watched. It is amazing and it has bery lovable characters and a fandom that is so toxic I really managed to make myself a part of an internet war on who of the characters is the best out of all and who should the main character end up with. It is an amazing time when it was still airing and it really is one of the best years of my 16 year old self.

    Looking at it objectively though, the series is quite generic and cliche but what is outstanding about this is that each character has time to develop the main girl, Chitoge, is a pain in the ass in the beginning but once you see her develop you will feel that yeah she is kinda cute and end up liking her. Onodera, The rival of Chitoge and Raku's first love, is someone who will grow on you. You will like her even if you support other girls who are in the race to claim Raku's heart. It is amazing what a series like this can bring once the story turns and proceeds. Though going back to that point, it does tend to be slow sometimes but if you are hooked enough you won't even notice it passing by. 

    However looking at it, most fans especially the newer ones might not find this as right up their alley. But chances are if you have seen alot of anime this is something to watch for you.
