AnoHana: The Flower we saw that Day

A review BY: Andrea De Dios

    Life is full of regrets and there are times when those regrets kept us from moving forward. But what if you're given a chance to get rid of them? Anohana is a story of how a deceased girl named menma tries to settle her wish in order for her to rest in peace. Menma died when she was still a child, causing the disband of their group and the abandoning of their secret base (playhouse). Moving on to the present, menma suddenly appeared in front of jinta, one of her childhood friends, and asks him to help her rest in peace. But for some reason, jinta is the only one who can interact with her. So jinta tried to fulfill her wish. He tried to guess what her wish is about but fails. So he asked for help from their other childhood friends and slowly the group was completed once again. , with one goal in mind and that is to fulfill menma's wish. The secret base became lively again, memories of the past came back. In the process of granting menma's wish, everyone started to reveal the feelings that they hid inside their chest, the burden, the pain and the suffering that they felt from the regrets, they revealed it all. Finally they were able to fulfill her wish and the time to say goodbye came near. They were able to play hide and seek one more time and was able to bid goodbye properly.

    They lived in regret and unconsciously stopped moving on from the death as it gravely affected them mentall and emotionally. Running or forgetting doesn't mean you already accepted but instead it only make your burden heavier and painful like what happend to the group. If Menma didn't show up, they wouldn't have closure or fix their friendship. Helping them to vent out their regrets and feelings somehow made them to move on and finally accepting everything happened, especially the early death of Menma. And that why anohana is a great story. The opening and ending song are great too
