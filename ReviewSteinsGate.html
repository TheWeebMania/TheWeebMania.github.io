Steins; Gate

A review by: Ruzchel Lloyd Estrella

    A story about time travel and the consequences of it's misuse. The protagonist is a man named Okabe Rintarou; a self-proclaimed "mad scientist" who goes by the name of Hououin Kyouma.

    Dumbfounded by this experience, he begins to piece together a successful method of time travel.

    Initially a slow paced story that you really need to pay attention to in order to understand the story. The common problem here is getting over the first few episodes without getting bored because for the first 11 episodes the series is still in the world building phase where Okabe just makes himself a time machine, with the help of Makise Kurisu, a world renowned scientist, and his friends, that can send emails to the past. Little did he know that by discovering such method would he be able to gather the attention of organizations hiding in the shadows just to stop him from doing any thing to the point that he gets his friends killed. He uses the time travel machine he created to make changes so he  can go back in time. Little did he know that what ever happens to you will always happen so he witness his friends get killed over and over until he finally snaps and understand the true mechanocs of time. There exist worlds with different endings and only one ending exists where all his friends are alive and well. That timeline is called the Steins; Gate Timeline and he is aiming to go there but it is not so simple as to changing something that you did in the past.

  The series has a really good interpretation of what time travel is supposed to be like. So it really shows the power of the phrase " With great power comes great responsibilities." Because time is not something you can mess with like a toy because once you do some it's hard to take back and a simple do over is not the correct answer to change something. 

   I personally really enjoy this series alot because of how it presented the concept of time travel which is very deep and complicated to understand. The show is quite slow to ramp up but once you get through that initial hurdle its all smooth sailing and awesomeness.

    Suggestion: After watching the final episode of the first season go back to episode one and everything will make sense.
