The JoJo Guide

By: Ruzchel Lloyd Estrella

The JoJo Franchise 

"Is that a motherf***ing JoJo reference?"  a phrase that is highly associated with the series itself and a phrase that also made the sereis such an icaon in the anime industry. This is caused by the many pop culture references coming from different bands and many more. Aside from this, JoJo also generated tons of memes starting from the to be continued meme and others such as the creepy "rero rero rero rero rero" scene of Noriaki Kakyoin. You just have to be surprised at what it has in store for you. 

So how do you exactly watch JoJo? for starters JoJo is dived into 8 parts and each part can be a stand alone series on its own. What happens is that in each part the main character of the story is a direct descendant of the Joestar bloodline in which the legacy starts with the very first JoJo Jonathan Joestar, wh is the main character for part one of the series. For now the only available adaptation of the anime is up until part five so the other three: can be consumed only by reading the manga.

Disclaimer: the shows that will be mentioned here are the adaptations of the studio called david Productions and will not be mentioning the older adaptations in order to avoid confusion.

1. Jojo's Bizarre Adventure(2012)

    The first adaptation done by dadvid production of the JoJo franchise which features part one and two of the original manga. The first part is lead by the very first JoJo Jonathan Joestar and his rivalry with the fiend Dio Brando, a boy adopted by the Joestars due to a debt. This is the very first part of how Dio and acquired super natural powers and how Jonathan stopped the evil brewing from the horrors that dio can release.

    Part two feature's Jonathan's grandson, Joseph Joestar and his genius. Practices fidhting techniques to defeat the new evil that has been dug underground by the Speedwagon Foundation, The Pillar Men, now awakens and threatens to destroy the earth. It is up to Joseph to stop them following the heroic exploits of his grandfather, Jonathan Joestar.

2. JoJo's Bizarre Adventures: Stardust Crusaders

The second anime adaptation features the first half of the third part which introduces to us the concept of Stands and is lead by the character name Kujo Jotaro, The grandson of Joseph Joestar, who is now out there to cure his sick mother along with his grandfather to defeat the reawakened Dio Brando who possess the ability to give the power of Stands to people. Along the way they meet allies who wants to help them beat Dio for ruining their lives and want revenge against him.

3. JoJo's Bizarre Adventure: Stardust Crusaders Battle In Egypt

This is the continuation of the first stardust crusader series where Jotaro and his allies now arrive in Egypt, the hideout of Dio and his henchmen. Our heroes now confront him and his best henchmen for the final showdown to determine the winner between good and evil.

4. JoJo's Bizzare Adventure Diamond is Unbreakable

The fourth anime adaptation which covers the entirety of part 4 in the manga. The main character here is named Higashikata Josuke, the son of Joseph Joestar when he met a girl in Japan. Now starts the antics as Josuke is now tracked by Jotaro because they belive that he developed a stand as well. Now begins the life of Josuke as strange series of killings and disappearances happen in his hometown of Morioh. Now it is up to them to find out who is causing such happenings and why does it happen.

5. JoJo's Bizarre Adventure: Golden Wind

    Part 5 of the original manga's adaptation features Giorno Giovanna, the son of Dio Brando when he handled Jonathan Joestars body. He lives in Italy where a mafia has been destroying the people of its country through drugs and obtaining power for themselves. Giorno now wants to put a stop to this with his friends and infiltrate the gang from the inside exposing the horrors done by the gang and destroying their leader. All in order to become the best mafia in Italy who exists to protect the people of its country 




    This ends the guide for this is the latest anime adaptation of the JoJo series and they still have 3 parts left to adapt: Stone Ocean, Rolling Stone, and Jojolion respectively. you can know there storyby reading the original manga written by the genius, Hirihiko Araki.
